You sign up for a support chat tool. The solo-founder plan is $29 a month — fine.
Six months later you bring in a VA to help answer questions while you're heads-down.
You add them to the workspace. The invoice becomes $58.

The VA works 10 hours a week. They handle maybe 15 tickets. You're paying $29 a month
for those 15 tickets because "seat" is how the vendor counts value, and one extra human
equals one extra seat.

That's the per-seat trap in its purest form.

## Why the model exists (and who it was built for)

Per-seat pricing makes sense when you're selling to a procurement team. A company with
50 support agents can be charged for 50 seats, the unit economics work, and the
customer's budget scales with headcount. Everyone's aligned.

Solo founders and tiny teams break this model. You might be:

- The only person talking to customers (1 seat, but you're doing the work of 5)
- Occasionally looping in a co-founder or contractor (now 2 seats, for a person who logs
  in twice a week)
- Running a product that does $2,000 MRR and paying $150/month for a support tool that
  has features you'll never use

The seat is a proxy for value. It just happens to be a terrible proxy for solo
builders.

## The real cost math

Let's say you run a small SaaS. You get 30–50 support conversations a month. You're on
Intercom's Essentials plan at $29/seat, plus you added their AI features — now you're
in the $74/seat tier.

- Month 1: just you. $74.
- Month 4: you add a part-time contractor. $148.
- Month 8: you hire a junior support person. $222.

Nothing changed about your *support volume* — still 40 conversations a month. But the
bill tripled because three humans have login credentials. You can read more about how
this plays out at scale in the [Krispy vs Intercom breakdown](/krispy-vs-intercom),
which goes through the numbers tier by tier.

At $222/month, you're spending $2,664 a year to handle 480 conversations. That's $5.55
per conversation. For a solo founder, that's a number worth sitting with.

## What you actually need

Here's what most solo founders actually need from a live chat tool:

1. A widget visitors can use
2. An AI layer that handles common questions automatically
3. A real human in the loop for when the AI can't help
4. A notification when someone needs you

No ticket queues. No CRM sync. No CSAT reports with 14 custom dimensions.

Enterprise support tools are priced for enterprise support teams. You're buying a
737 because it has seats, when what you need is a good bicycle.

## The lock-in you don't notice until you need to leave

There's a second cost baked into most per-seat tools: data ownership.

Your conversation history lives on their servers. Your customer context is in their
schema. The widget on your site pings their CDN. None of this feels like a problem
until you want to export three years of threads, their pricing changes, or a
privacy-conscious customer asks where their data goes.

## FAQ

**Why does per-seat pricing hurt solo founders specifically?**

Per-seat pricing scales with headcount, not with the value a tool delivers. Solo
founders do the work of many roles, so the bill grows the moment they bring in any
help — a contractor, a VA, a co-founder with occasional access. A 40-conversation
month costs the same whether one person or four people handled it; only the seat count
changes. That mismatch is specific to small teams.

**What's a fair alternative to per-seat pricing for support tools?**

Usage-based pricing (per conversation or per resolution) aligns cost with actual
support volume. Self-hosted open-source tools are another option: you pay for
infrastructure, not per human who has a login. Krispy is free to self-host on
Cloudflare Workers — your only cost is your Cloudflare account, which covers most
small projects on the free tier.

**Can I still have multiple team members without paying per-seat?**

With Krispy, yes. The Telegram handoff means multiple people can receive and respond to
conversations without each needing a billed seat. Add as many people to the Telegram
bot or group as you want — the infra cost is flat. For SaaS tools, look for plans that
charge per workspace or per volume rather than per user.

**How much does per-seat pricing actually cost a solo founder over a year?**

At $29–74/seat/month for common tools, a two-person setup runs $696–$1,776 annually.
A three-person team (you, a VA, a co-founder) hits $1,044–$2,664 a year. If your
product does a few thousand dollars MRR, that support bill is a meaningful slice of
margin — before you've integrated anything or added AI features.

**Is self-hosting actually simpler than paying for SaaS?**
