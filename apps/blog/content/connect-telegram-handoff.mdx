You're at a coffee shop. Someone opens a chat on your site, the bot handles the first
few messages fine, then they type "can I talk to a real person?" Your phone buzzes.
You reply in Telegram. The visitor sees your message instantly. You never opened a
dashboard, never switched apps, never logged into anything.

That's the handoff. Here's how it actually works.

## The short version

Krispy runs a Cloudflare Durable Object that owns each live conversation — state, history,
the works. When a handoff is triggered (visitor request, keyword match, or a confidence
threshold the bot doesn't clear), the DO fires a message to a Telegram bot you control.
Your reply goes back through the same bot, the DO routes it into the open WebSocket, and
the visitor sees it in the chat widget. One conversation object, two participants, one
channel that lives in your pocket.

If you want the deeper architecture behind why a Durable Object is the right primitive
here, the [Durable Objects post](/how-we-built-live-takeover-durable-objects) covers it
— the short answer is that a single serialized object gives you exactly-once delivery
and zero message drops when the handoff happens mid-conversation.

## Setting it up

You need two things: a Telegram bot token and a chat ID to deliver notifications to.
Both take about three minutes to get.

1. Open [@BotFather](https://t.me/botfather) in Telegram, run `/newbot`, grab the token.
2. Start a conversation with your new bot (or add it to a team group), then call
   `https://api.telegram.org/bot<TOKEN>/getUpdates` to find the `chat.id`.
3. Add both to your Krispy Worker secrets:

```sh
wrangler secret put TELEGRAM_BOT_TOKEN
wrangler secret put TELEGRAM_CHAT_ID
```

That's it. Redeploy and Krispy will route handoff notifications to that chat. If you're
team-based, `TELEGRAM_CHAT_ID` can be a group chat — everyone in the group gets the
ping and the first person to reply wins the conversation. No extra config, just group chat
semantics doing the work.

If you haven't deployed Krispy yet, the [5-minute setup guide](/add-live-chat-in-5-minutes)
walks through the full `wrangler deploy` flow from zero.

## What the notification looks like

When a handoff fires, your Telegram gets something like:

```
🥐 Krispy — handoff requested
Site: yoursite.com
Visitor: Session #a4f2 (3 messages)

"Hi, I'd like to talk to someone about enterprise pricing"

— Reply to this message to respond
```

Replying to that Telegram thread sends your message back to the visitor. The widget shows
it with your name (or "Support" — configurable). The bot's turn indicator disappears, and
the visitor knows they're talking to a human. If you close the Telegram thread without
replying, the DO keeps the conversation open on your end; nothing drops.

## The conversation the visitor already had stays intact

This is the part that usually surprises people: when you take over, the visitor's screen
doesn't flicker, there's no "a human will be with you shortly" dead air, and they don't
have to repeat themselves. The Durable Object has the full transcript. Your first reply
arrives in the same thread, in sequence.

On your Telegram side, the notification message includes the prior context — not a
summary, the actual messages — so you can read up before you respond. The flow from
the visitor's perspective and from your perspective is covered in detail in
[Bot to human without dropping the conversation](/bot-to-human-without-dropping-the-ball).

## FAQ

### Can I really reply from just the Telegram app, no other login needed?

Yes. Once the handoff notification arrives in Telegram, you reply to that specific message
thread and Krispy routes it back. You never need the Krispy dashboard open. The dashboard
exists for history, analytics, and configuration — not for active conversations if you're
on the go.

### What triggers the handoff — does the visitor have to ask, or does it happen automatically?

Both. A visitor typing "talk to a human", "agent", "real person" (or your own keyword list)
triggers it explicitly. You can also set a confidence threshold: if the bot produces a
low-confidence answer two turns in a row, it hands off automatically before the visitor
has to ask. Both triggers are configured in your `krispy.config.ts`.

### What happens to the chat history when I take over?

Nothing is wiped. The Durable Object accumulates every message regardless of who sends it.
When you take over, the transcript is continuous — bot messages, visitor messages, your
replies — all in order. The visitor's widget shows the same thread throughout.

### Can multiple people on my team receive the Telegram notification?

Yes. Point `TELEGRAM_CHAT_ID` at a group chat that includes your whole support team.
Everyone sees the handoff notification. The first person to reply claims the conversation —
subsequent replies from others in the group are ignored until the conversation closes. No
race conditions; the DO handles exactly-once delivery.

### What if I'm unavailable and don't reply? Does the visitor get stuck?

No. You can configure a fallback: a timeout message the bot sends ("We're a little busy
right now — expect a reply within a few hours"), or a re-engagement to the bot for
simple queries while you're away. The conversation stays open in the DO. When you do
reply in Telegram — even hours later — it delivers to the visitor if they're still in the
tab, or waits for their next visit.
