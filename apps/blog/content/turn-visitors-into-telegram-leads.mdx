Most "lead capture" advice assumes you have someone watching a dashboard. If you're building a side project or a small product, you don't — you're also writing the code, posting about it, and occasionally sleeping.

Here's what actually happens: a visitor lands on your site at 11pm, reads your pricing page, and types "does this integrate with Notion?" Nobody answers. They close the tab. You find out about it never.

That's not a conversion problem. It's a presence problem. The visitor was there, had a real question, and you had no idea.

Krispy fixes this without requiring you to staff a chat shift. The AI handles the conversation while you're away — answers what it knows, keeps the visitor engaged — and when something worth your attention happens, you get a Telegram message with the full thread. You reply from your phone. The visitor sees your response in the chat widget. You never opened a dashboard.

## How the lead capture loop works

When a visitor starts a chat, Krispy's AI takes the first turn. It answers from the context you gave it (your docs, your FAQ, a system prompt), qualifies the visitor if you set it up to, and keeps things moving.

When the conversation reaches a point worth a human — the visitor asks for you directly, mentions pricing, or hits a question the AI can't answer — Krispy fires a Telegram notification with the entire thread attached. No summary, no paraphrase: the real conversation, word for word.

You reply in Telegram. That reply appears in the widget on your site in seconds. The visitor doesn't know you were on your phone on a bus; they just got an answer.

The [full mechanics of the human handoff](/connect-telegram-handoff) are covered separately, including how to configure what triggers a notification and how to take over from the AI mid-conversation. The short version: you're in control of when you appear, and the AI covers the gaps.

Every conversation is logged. Even if you don't reply, you have a record of who visited, what they asked, and how the AI responded. That's your lead list.

## Setting it up

If you want to run this yourself, you need two things: Krispy deployed to Cloudflare Workers, and a Telegram bot connected to it. The [5-minute deploy guide](/add-live-chat-in-5-minutes) covers the Cloudflare side. Here's the Telegram piece.

Create a bot with BotFather — `/newbot`, follow the prompts, copy the token. Then:

```bash
wrangler secret put TELEGRAM_BOT_TOKEN
# paste your bot token when prompted

wrangler secret put TELEGRAM_CHAT_ID
# your personal Telegram user ID, or a group/channel ID
```

That's the integration. Krispy uses the bot token to send conversation threads to your chat and to relay your replies back to the widget.

If you're running multiple projects, give each one its own Telegram group. Add your bot to each group, grab the group's chat ID with `/getUpdates`, and set it per deployment. Leads from each site land in their own stream.

## Pre-qualifying visitors automatically

Here's where this gets useful beyond just "chat widget that texts me." You can configure Krispy's system prompt to collect information before the handoff, so every lead that hits your Telegram comes with context.

Example system prompt addition:

```
Before routing to a human, ask the visitor:
1. What they're trying to build or solve
2. Whether they're a solo founder or part of a team

Include their answers in the handoff summary.
```

Now every Telegram notification reads something like: "Building an internal tool for a 4-person agency, wants to know about white-labeling." You know what to say before you type a word. The AI did the first-pass qualification; you do the close.

## FAQ

**How does Krispy route visitor chats to Telegram?**

Krispy connects via a Telegram bot you create with BotFather. When a visitor chats on your site, the conversation is forwarded to your Telegram chat ID. You reply in Telegram; Krispy sends your reply back to the widget in real time. Setup takes about five minutes and requires two `wrangler secret put` commands.

**Can I capture a visitor's email through the chat widget?**

Yes — configure Krispy's AI to ask for it during the conversation. "What email should I use to follow up?" sits naturally in a chat context and converts better than a form field. The visitor's answer is part of the thread you receive in Telegram. There's no built-in CRM push, but the thread is a durable record you can act on.

**What if I miss the Telegram notification?**

The conversation stays in Krispy's database. You can reply later — the visitor will see your response if they return to the site. The AI continues fielding new messages in the meantime, so the conversation doesn't go cold just because you were slow.

**Does this work across multiple sites?**

Yes. Deploy Krispy once per site, point each deployment at a different `TELEGRAM_CHAT_ID` — your own Telegram, a group per project, a channel for a team. Each site's leads land in their own stream.

**What triggers the Telegram notification — every message or only some?**

By default, Krispy notifies you when a visitor asks for a human or when the conversation has enough signal to be worth your time. You can tune this: notify on every new conversation (noisier, useful early on) or hold until the AI decides a handoff is warranted (quieter, better at scale). The threshold lives in your system prompt config.
