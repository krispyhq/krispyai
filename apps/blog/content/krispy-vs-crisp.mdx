We have nothing against Crisp. It is a good product. The problem is that at some point
you open the billing page, do the math on seats plus the AI add-on, and realize you are
paying somewhere between $100 and $400 a month for a widget that sits on your site and
a database full of your customer conversations that you cannot take with you.

That is the itch Krispy exists to scratch.

## What Krispy actually is

Krispy is an open-source live chat with a built-in AI layer that runs on Cloudflare
Workers AI — no third-party API key required. When a visitor asks something, the bot
answers from your knowledge base. When it cannot, or when the visitor wants a real human,
the conversation hands off to Telegram so the team member nearest their phone can pick
it up. The whole thing self-hosts on Cloudflare's free tier.

You can also skip the infra entirely and use Krispy Cloud, which has a free tier for
small teams who want the same stack without running `wrangler deploy`.

## How the two compare

| | **Krispy** | **Crisp** |
|---|---|---|
| Open-source | Yes (MIT) | No |
| Self-hostable | Yes — Cloudflare Workers | No |
| AI replies built-in | Yes — Workers AI, no key | Add-on ($, separate plan) |
| Human handoff to your phone | Yes — Telegram | Inbox (web/app only) |
| Per-seat cost | $0 to self-host | $25–95/seat/mo |
| Data ownership | Your infra, your data | Crisp's servers |

The per-seat cost line is the obvious one. But the data ownership row changes your risk
profile. Crisp's terms let you export conversations, but the database lives on their
servers. Outage? You feel it. Moving? You negotiate an export. With Krispy, the data is
wherever you deployed it.

## Spinning up Krispy takes one command

```bash
npx create-krispy-app my-chat
cd my-chat
wrangler deploy
```

That deploys the Workers API, creates the KV namespace for your knowledge base, and
wires the Telegram webhook in one shot. The widget script drops into your site as a
single `<script>` tag. There is no server to babysit — Cloudflare handles scaling,
cold starts, and the edge routing.

## The AI layer: what "no key required" actually means

Crisp's AI features run on OpenAI under the hood. That is a fine choice, but it means
you need an OpenAI account, a usage cap, and a separate billing relationship on top
of your Crisp subscription. Krispy's AI runs on Workers AI (Cloudflare's inference
platform), which is included in the Workers free tier up to 10,000 neurons per day.
For most small to mid-size products that is enough to answer every inbound question
with zero marginal cost.

The model Krispy ships with is `@cf/meta/llama-3-8b-instruct`. You can swap it to
any model in the Workers AI catalog by changing one line in `krispy.config.ts`:

```ts
// krispy.config.ts
export default {
  ai: { model: "@cf/mistral/mistral-7b-instruct-v0.1" },
};
```

## The handoff: why Telegram

Crisp has a polished inbox — desktop, mobile app, email notifications. It works well
if your team lives in that tab. Most small teams do not. They are in Slack, or iMessage,
or in Telegram with their co-founders already.

Krispy bets on Telegram because it is where the humans already are. When a visitor
escalates, a Telegram bot pings the on-call person directly. They reply in Telegram and
the message appears in the chat widget on your site. No app to install, no context switch.

It is not as polished as Crisp's inbox. If your team needs assignment queues, canned
responses, and first-reply analytics, Krispy is not there yet — and we will say so
plainly. For a founder who wants to know the moment a visitor asks "how do I pay" and
respond from their phone, it is exactly right.

## What you give up

Fair is fair. Crisp has years of UI polish, a mobile app, integrations with HubSpot and
Slack, and a team that works on it full-time. Krispy is newer and scrappier.

The things Krispy does not have today:

- A dedicated mobile app (Telegram is the handoff; there is no Krispy mobile)
- A visual inbox with assignment and tagging
- Pre-built CRM integrations

If those gaps are dealbreakers, Crisp or one of the other options in
[the best open-source live chat alternatives in 2026](/best-open-source-live-chat)
might suit your situation better. There is no shame in that.

## FAQ

### Is Krispy a real Crisp alternative or just a side project?

Krispy is MIT-licensed, runs on Cloudflare's global edge, and handles AI replies, human
handoff via Telegram, and a self-hosted knowledge base. Whether it fits depends on how
much you value inbox UX versus data ownership and cost — both are real trade-offs.

### Can I migrate my Crisp conversation history to Krispy?

Crisp lets you export conversations as JSON. Krispy has an importer that loads that
export into your KV namespace so your support history moves with you. Not a one-click
wizard yet, but a documented script — no manual copy-paste.

### Do I need to pay for AI features when self-hosting?

No. Krispy runs on Cloudflare Workers AI, included in the free tier up to 10,000
inference requests per day. No OpenAI key, no separate AI billing. Beyond the free cap,
Cloudflare's pay-as-you-go applies — roughly $0.01 per 1,000 requests.

### Who owns my chat data when I self-host Krispy?

You do, completely. Conversations are stored in Cloudflare KV under your own account.
Krispy (the project) never receives, processes, or stores any of your visitor data.
Your Cloudflare account, your data, your terms.

### Is there a hosted version if I don't want to run infrastructure?

Yes — Krispy Cloud. It runs the same open-source stack and has a free tier for up to
500 conversations per month. The code powering it is the same repo you can deploy
yourself, so you are never locked in: export your data and self-host any time.
