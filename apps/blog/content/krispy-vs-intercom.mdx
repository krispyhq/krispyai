The first time I added a third person to our Intercom workspace, the invoice jumped $74.
Not $74 total — $74 *more*, per month, for one extra seat. Nobody warned me. It's just
how per-seat pricing works: invisible while the team is small, then it grows with you,
in the wrong direction.

That math is the reason Krispy exists.

## What Intercom actually costs

To be fair: Intercom is a genuinely good product. The shared inbox is polished, the AI
Copilot is useful, and Fin (their AI agent) works well for common questions. But the
pricing has a shape that hurts small teams trying to grow.

Their Essentials plan starts at $29/seat/month. Add the AI features worth having — Fin,
Copilot — and you're on a higher tier. A two-person support team on the current
AI-first stack lands around $150–250/month. Add a third seat and a couple of add-ons
and you're at $300–400 before you've integrated anything.

That's not an indictment of their value. It's just the math. For a funded startup with
a 10-person support team, $400/mo is a rounding error. For a solo founder doing their
own support, or a small agency adding AI chat to a client site, it's the wrong shape
entirely. We wrote more about why this structure has such a specific sting in
[Why per-seat pricing punishes solo founders](/why-per-seat-pricing-hurts-solo-founders).

## What you trade away with a SaaS chat tool

The per-seat bill is the obvious cost. The less obvious one is ownership.

When your chat runs on Intercom's infrastructure, your conversation history lives there.
Your customer data lives there. The widget on your site is a call to their CDN. None of
this is a problem until it is — until the pricing changes, until you need to export
three years of support threads, or until GDPR becomes a meaningful concern for your
region.

Self-hosting solves this cleanly. You own the data, the schema, the uptime decisions.
And with Krispy running on Cloudflare Workers, "self-hosting" doesn't mean a VPS you
SSH into at 2am. It means `wrangler deploy`.

## How Krispy works

Krispy is open-source AI live chat with a human-in-the-loop handoff. The AI runs on
Cloudflare Workers AI — no separate API key, no per-token bill for the model. When a
visitor's question needs a real person, the message routes to Telegram: it lands on
your phone, you reply, and the visitor sees the response in the widget in real time.
Your support inbox is wherever you already are.

Getting started looks like this:

```bash
git clone https://github.com/krispyai/krispy
cd krispy
cp .env.example .env   # add your Telegram bot token + Cloudflare account ID
wrangler deploy
```

🥐 Let the bot cook from there. No seats, no monthly vendor bill, no renegotiation
when you hire person number two.

## FAQ

**Is there a free version of Intercom?**

Intercom offers a 14-day trial but no permanent free tier. Their cheapest paid plan
starts at $29/seat/month billed annually. For persistent free live chat, you need an
open-source alternative. Krispy is self-hosted on Cloudflare and costs $0 in platform
fees — you pay only for your Cloudflare account, which covers most small projects on
the free tier.

**Can I self-host Intercom?**

No. Intercom is closed-source SaaS with no self-hosting option. Your data and
conversation history live on their infrastructure and can't be moved without an export.
Krispy is the self-hosted alternative — it runs entirely on your Cloudflare account,
so you own the data from day one.

**Does Krispy have AI like Intercom's Fin?**

Yes. AI chat is built in via Cloudflare Workers AI, which requires no external API key.
It answers common questions automatically and hands off to a human (via Telegram) when
it can't help. It doesn't yet match Fin's depth on complex support flows, but for most
indie and small-team use cases it handles the first-response layer well.

**What's the catch with self-hosting?**

You own the maintenance. When Krispy ships an update you pull it and redeploy — no
automatic upgrades, no support tier. Cloudflare Workers removes the infrastructure ops,
but you're still the operator. If you'd rather not manage that, Krispy Cloud's free
tier handles the hosting.

**How does the Telegram handoff actually work?**

When a visitor's question needs a human, Krispy sends the conversation to a Telegram
bot linked to your account. You reply in Telegram and the visitor sees the message
appear in the chat widget instantly. No second screen to monitor, no separate inbox to
open — just your phone, which you already carry.
