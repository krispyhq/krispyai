Both tools let you own your chat data. The real question is what you want to own alongside it: a mature multi-channel support platform backed by a full Rails stack, or a leaner AI-first widget that runs on Cloudflare and pings you on Telegram when a visitor needs a human. That's the actual fork in the road.

## What Chatwoot is good at

Chatwoot is a real product and it earns its reputation. You get a shared team inbox, email, WhatsApp, Facebook Messenger, Twitter DMs, and API channels — all routing into one web dashboard your whole support team logs into. Labels, canned responses, SLAs, CSAT surveys. It's been in production for years and it shows.

If you're running a five-person support team that lives in a browser tab and handles volume across multiple channels, Chatwoot is a serious option. The self-hosted MIT version is genuinely free and the community is active.

## What Chatwoot costs to run

Here's where the comparison gets concrete. Chatwoot self-hosting isn't just a binary — it's a stack:

- A Ruby on Rails app
- Sidekiq workers (background jobs)
- Redis
- PostgreSQL

A production-grade setup on a single $40/mo VPS is workable for light traffic, but you'll want managed Postgres ($15–25/mo) and at least a second node for reliability as you grow. Realistically, you're looking at **$60–120/mo** before any cloud support tier. Chatwoot Cloud starts at $19 per seat per month — five agents is $95/mo.

Krispy self-hosted on Cloudflare Workers costs $0. The free tier covers most small and mid-sized sites comfortably, and Workers AI (the built-in inference that powers Krispy's bot) is included. You deploy once:

```bash
npx wrangler deploy
```

That's it. No server to patch, no Redis to babysit, no Sidekiq queue to monitor.

We go deeper on the infrastructure side in [Self-host live chat on Cloudflare for free](/self-host-live-chat-cloudflare) if you want the full walkthrough.

## The AI and handoff story

Chatwoot doesn't ship with AI. There are community integrations and webhooks you can wire to OpenAI yourself, but it's not built in — you're assembling the pieces.

Krispy's bot runs on Cloudflare Workers AI, which means no OpenAI API key, no usage bill from a third-party model provider, and no cold starts in a Lambda somewhere. The model runs at the edge, collocated with your Worker. When the bot hits its limit — a question it can't answer, a visitor who explicitly asks for a human — it hands off to Telegram. Your phone buzzes, you pick up the conversation, you close the ticket. That handoff is native, not a webhook you wire yourself.

This is a narrower feature surface than Chatwoot's shared inbox. It's designed for a solo founder or a small team where one person is the support function. If you need five agents triaging queues in a web dashboard, Chatwoot is the right pick.

## When to pick Chatwoot

Pick Chatwoot if you need to centralize email + social + chat into one team inbox, your support load justifies dedicated agents, and you're comfortable running a small backend. It's a proven platform and the free self-hosted tier is genuinely usable.

## When to pick Krispy 🥐

Pick Krispy if you want AI handling first contact automatically, you don't want to manage a server, and you or a small team are the humans behind the chat. ## FAQ

### Is Krispy a drop-in replacement for Chatwoot?

Not a drop-in, no. Krispy is narrower: web chat only, no email or social channels, and human handoff goes to Telegram rather than a web inbox. If you rely on Chatwoot's multi-channel routing or shared team queues, you'd lose those. If you mainly use Chatwoot for a chat widget and a bot, Krispy covers that ground with less overhead.

### Can I self-host Krispy without any cloud account at all?

You need a Cloudflare account, but the free plan is enough for most sites. Workers, KV, and Workers AI inference all fall within the free tier limits — no credit card required to deploy. Compare that to Chatwoot, which needs a VPS with at least 2 GB RAM to run Rails and Sidekiq comfortably.

### Does Chatwoot run on Cloudflare Workers?

No. Chatwoot is a Ruby on Rails application and requires a traditional server environment (VPS or container) with Postgres, Redis, and Sidekiq. It's not designed for serverless edge runtimes. Krispy is built specifically for Workers from the ground up.

### What AI model does Krispy use, and can I swap it?

Krispy defaults to a model available on Cloudflare Workers AI — you don't configure a key or pay a third-party provider. The model runs at the edge alongside your Worker. You can swap the model by changing one config value; the full list of supported Workers AI text models is in the Cloudflare docs.

### Is Krispy suitable if I already use Chatwoot and just want an AI layer?

Krispy isn't a plugin for Chatwoot — it's a separate widget. If your team is already set up in Chatwoot's inbox and you just want AI on top, the easier path is wiring Chatwoot's agent bot webhooks to an LLM. Krispy makes more sense as an alternative if you're starting fresh or the Chatwoot stack feels heavier than what your use case justifies.
